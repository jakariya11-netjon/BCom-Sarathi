Consumer Affairs

Unit 1 & Unit 4 – Full Questions & Answers

Unit 1

1 Mark

Q1. Define Consumer.
A consumer is a person who purchases goods or services for personal use and not for resale or business purposes.
Q2. What is Market?
A market is a place where buyers and sellers interact to exchange goods and services at agreed prices.
Q3. What is MRP?
MRP is the maximum price printed on a product that can be legally charged from consumers.
Q4. What is Fair Price?
Fair price is a reasonable price charged for goods based on cost, quality, and consumer interest without exploitation.
Q5. What is Labelling?
Labelling means providing important information like price, expiry date, and manufacturer details on the product package.
Q6. What is Packaging?
Packaging refers to covering goods for protection, safe transport, storage, and also to attract consumers.
Q7. What is Consumer Complaint?
A consumer complaint is a formal expression of dissatisfaction regarding defective goods or poor services.
Q8. What is Consumer Satisfaction?
Consumer satisfaction is the feeling when a product or service meets or exceeds expectations.

4 Mark

Q1. Explain the concept and nature of markets.
A market is a system where buyers and sellers interact to exchange goods and services. It operates through demand and supply forces which determine prices. Markets may be local, national, or global and can function physically or online. The nature of markets includes competition, price mechanism, and availability of goods. A well-organized market ensures efficient distribution, promotes fair trade practices, and satisfies consumer needs. It also helps producers reach a large number of consumers easily.
Q2. Discuss MRP and Local Taxes.
MRP or Maximum Retail Price is the highest price that can be charged from consumers for a product. It includes all taxes such as GST, so no extra charge is allowed. MRP ensures transparency in pricing and protects consumers from overcharging. Local taxes are already included in the printed price, making it easier for consumers to know the final cost. This system promotes fairness in trade and prevents exploitation, while building trust between buyers and sellers.
Q3. Explain Labelling and Packaging.
Labelling provides important information about a product such as price, ingredients, manufacturing date, expiry date, and instructions for use. It helps consumers make informed decisions. Packaging involves covering and protecting goods for safe storage and transportation. It also improves the appearance of the product and attracts customers. Both labelling and packaging are essential for safety, marketing, and legal compliance. Proper labelling ensures transparency, while effective packaging maintains product quality and convenience.
Q4. Describe Consumer Dissatisfaction and Complaints.
Consumer dissatisfaction arises when a product or service fails to meet expectations. This may occur due to poor quality, defects, misleading advertisements, or inadequate service. Complaints are formal expressions of dissatisfaction made to sellers or authorities. They help in resolving issues and improving product and service quality. An effective complaint system ensures quick redressal and builds consumer confidence. It also encourages businesses to maintain better standards and accountability.
Q5. Explain Internal and External Complaint Handling System.
Internal complaint handling refers to resolving consumer complaints within the organization through customer service departments. It is usually quick and cost-free. External complaint handling involves consumer courts, ombudsman, or regulatory bodies when internal methods fail. These systems provide legal support and ensure justice for consumers. Both systems are essential for protecting consumer rights and maintaining transparency, accountability, and trust in the business environment.

Unit 4

1 Mark

Q1. What is RBI?
Reserve Bank of India is the central bank that regulates the banking system, controls monetary policy, and protects consumer interests.
Q2. What is Banking Ombudsman?
Banking Ombudsman is a scheme introduced by RBI to resolve customer complaints related to banking services quickly and without cost.
Q3. What is IRDA?
IRDA is the regulatory authority that supervises and develops the insurance sector and ensures protection of policyholders’ interests.
Q4. What is Insurance Ombudsman?
Insurance Ombudsman is an authority that resolves complaints of policyholders against insurance companies in a simple and cost-effective manner.
Q5. What is TRAI?
TRAI is a regulatory body that controls telecom services, ensures fair pricing, and protects consumer interests in communication sector.
Q6. What is FSSAI?
FSSAI is an authority that sets food safety standards and ensures availability of safe, hygienic, and quality food products to consumers.
Q7. What is ASCI?
ASCI is a self-regulatory organization that monitors advertisements and ensures they are truthful, fair, and not misleading to consumers.
Q8. What is Electricity Regulatory Commission?
Electricity Regulatory Commission is a body that regulates electricity supply, fixes tariffs, and ensures fair and reliable services for consumers.

The Banking Ombudsman Scheme is introduced by RBI to resolve complaints related to banking services such as delays, unfair charges, and service deficiencies in a simple, quick, and cost-free manner.

FSSAI is responsible for setting food safety standards, regulating food businesses, and ensuring the availability of safe and hygienic food products to consumers across the country.

4 Mark

Q1. Explain the role of RBI and Banking Ombudsman. (4 Marks)
Reserve Bank of India (RBI) regulates the banking system and ensures fair practices to protect consumers. It issues guidelines, supervises banks, and maintains financial stability. The Banking Ombudsman, introduced by RBI, resolves customer complaints related to banking services such as delays, unfair charges, and poor service. It provides a simple, quick, and cost-free mechanism for grievance redressal. Together, RBI and the Banking Ombudsman play an important role in ensuring transparency, accountability, and protection of consumers in the banking sector.
Q2. Explain IRDA and Insurance Ombudsman. (4 Marks)
IRDA regulates and supervises the insurance sector to ensure transparency and protect policyholders’ interests. It monitors insurance companies and promotes fair practices in the industry. The Insurance Ombudsman resolves disputes between policyholders and insurers regarding claims, delays, or unfair practices. It provides a simple, quick, and cost-effective grievance redressal system. Both IRDA and the Insurance Ombudsman help in maintaining trust, fairness, and efficiency in the insurance sector while safeguarding consumer rights.
Q3. Explain the functions of TRAI. (4 Marks)
TRAI regulates the telecom sector in India and ensures fair and transparent functioning of service providers. It fixes tariffs, maintains service quality, and promotes healthy competition among telecom companies. TRAI also protects consumer interests by addressing complaints and ensuring proper communication services. It sets standards for call quality and internet services. By regulating telecom activities, TRAI plays a key role in ensuring efficient, reliable, and affordable communication services for consumers.
Q4. Describe the role of FSSAI in food safety. (4 Marks)
FSSAI ensures that food products available in the market are safe, hygienic, and of good quality. It sets standards for food production, storage, and distribution. It regulates food businesses and monitors compliance with safety norms. FSSAI also conducts inspections and takes action against violations. It creates awareness among consumers regarding food safety. Through these functions, FSSAI plays an important role in protecting public health and ensuring availability of safe food for consumers.
Q5. Explain the role of ASCI in controlling advertisements. (4 Marks)
ASCI is a self-regulatory body that ensures advertisements are truthful, fair, and not misleading. It monitors advertising practices and takes action against false or harmful advertisements. ASCI protects consumers from deceptive claims and promotes ethical standards in advertising. It also provides a platform for resolving complaints related to advertisements. By maintaining honesty and transparency, ASCI helps in building consumer trust and ensuring responsible advertising practices in the market.
