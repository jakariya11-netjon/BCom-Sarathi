🔹 2 Mark Questions

  • Define International Business with example.
    International business refers to buying, selling, and investment activities that take place across national borders between two or more countries. It includes export, import, and foreign investment. For example, India exporting tea or spices to the UK is a common international business activity.
  • State two features of International Business.
    International business involves transactions between multiple countries and requires the use of foreign currencies for payments. It also faces political, legal, and cultural differences among nations, making it more complex and risky compared to domestic business operations.
  • Difference between Export and Import.
    Export refers to selling goods and services from one country to another, whereas import means purchasing goods and services from foreign countries. Both are important elements of international trade and help in improving economic development and availability of goods.
  • What is Globalisation? Mention two impacts.
    Globalisation is the process of increasing economic integration and interdependence among countries through trade, investment, and technology. It leads to higher economic growth and better market access but also increases competition and dependence among nations.
  • Explain Comparative Advantage briefly.
    Comparative advantage refers to the ability of a country to produce goods at a lower opportunity cost compared to other countries. It encourages specialization in production, efficient allocation of resources, and promotes mutual benefits through international trade.
  • What is Foreign Exchange Market?
    Foreign exchange market is a global market where currencies of different countries are bought and sold. It facilitates international trade and investment by enabling currency conversion and helps in determining exchange rates based on demand and supply.
  • State two benefits of Regional Economic Co-operation.
    Regional economic cooperation reduces trade barriers such as tariffs and quotas, promoting free flow of goods and services. It also enhances economic growth, improves relations among countries, and allows sharing of resources, technology, and knowledge.
  • What is FDI? Mention two types.
    .Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) refers to investment made by a foreign company or individual in business operations of another country. It has two main types: inward FDI, where investment enters a country, and outward FDI, where investment goes abroad.
  • Write two functions of IMF.
    The International Monetary Fund (IMF) provides financial assistance to member countries facing balance of payment difficulties. It also helps in stabilizing exchange rates and promotes international monetary cooperation and global financial stability.
  • What is EU?
    European Union (EU) is a regional organization of European countries that promotes economic integration, free trade, and common policies among member nations. It aims to strengthen cooperation, improve economic growth, and ensure stability within the region.

🔹 4 Mark Questions

  • Explain the features of International Business.
    International business involves economic activities across national boundaries. It includes multiple countries, use of foreign currencies, and exposure to political and legal differences. Businesses must deal with cultural diversity, different consumer preferences, and varying regulations. It is more complex than domestic business due to risks like exchange rate fluctuations and government restrictions. It also requires knowledge of international markets and trade policies. Despite these challenges, it offers opportunities for expansion, higher profits, and access to global resources.
  • Distinguish between International Business and Domestic Business.
    International business takes place between two or more countries, whereas domestic business is conducted within a single country. IB involves multiple currencies, higher risks, and complex regulations, while domestic business uses a single currency and simpler rules. Cultural and legal differences are major challenges in IB but not in domestic trade. Transportation and communication costs are also higher in IB. However, IB provides access to larger markets and global opportunities, while domestic business is limited to national boundaries.
  • Explain the Absolute Advantage Theory.
    The Absolute Advantage Theory was developed by Adam Smith. It states that a country should specialize in producing goods in which it has an absolute efficiency over other countries. This means producing goods at lower cost or with fewer resources. By specializing and trading, countries can benefit from increased productivity and efficient resource use. It promotes international trade by encouraging countries to focus on their strengths. However, it does not explain trade when one country has advantage in all goods.
  • Explain the Comparative Advantage Theory.
    The Comparative Advantage Theory was given by David Ricardo. It suggests that countries should specialize in producing goods in which they have the lowest opportunity cost, even if they do not have an absolute advantage. This leads to efficient allocation of resources and mutual benefits from trade. By focusing on relative efficiency, countries can gain from specialization and exchange goods with others. This theory forms the basis of modern international trade and explains how trade can benefit all participating countries.
  • Explain the benefits of Regional Economic Co-operation.
    Regional economic cooperation helps countries to improve trade and economic relations. It reduces trade barriers like tariffs and quotas, making goods cheaper and more accessible. It promotes economic growth, investment, and employment opportunities among member countries. It also encourages sharing of technology, resources, and knowledge. Cooperation improves political relations and stability in the region. Examples include EU, ASEAN, and SAARC. Overall, it helps member nations to achieve faster development and strengthen their global position.
  • Discuss SAARC or ASEAN.
    SAARC and ASEAN are regional organizations formed to promote economic and political cooperation among member countries. SAARC includes South Asian countries, while ASEAN includes Southeast Asian nations. These organizations aim to improve trade, reduce poverty, and enhance regional stability. They encourage cooperation in areas like education, technology, and infrastructure. By reducing trade barriers and promoting integration, they help member countries grow economically and strengthen their relationships in the global market.
  • Explain the Foreign Exchange Market.
    The Foreign Exchange Market is a global marketplace where currencies of different countries are bought and sold. It plays a vital role in international trade and investment by facilitating currency conversion. It helps determine exchange rates based on demand and supply. The market operates 24 hours worldwide and includes banks, financial institutions, and governments. It also helps in managing exchange rate risks. Without the forex market, international transactions would be difficult to conduct.
  • Explain Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in India.
    Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in India refers to investment made by foreign companies or individuals in Indian businesses. It brings capital, advanced technology, and management skills, contributing to economic growth. FDI creates employment opportunities and improves infrastructure. The Indian government regulates FDI through policies and allows investment in various sectors. Initiatives like “Make in India” encourage foreign investors. However, some sectors have restrictions. Overall, FDI plays an important role in India’s development.
